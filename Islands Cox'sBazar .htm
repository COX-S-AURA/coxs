Cox's Bazar Islands: A Guide for Travelers

Discover the natural beauty and cultural diversity of Bangladesh's coastal islands

Introduction

Cox's Bazar is a district in southeastern Bangladesh, famous for its long sandy beach and scenic fishing villages. But beyond the mainland, there are also several islands that offer a unique experience for adventurous travelers. These islands are rich in biodiversity, cultural heritage, and local charm. In this document, we will introduce you to some of the most popular and accessible islands in Cox's Bazar, and give you some tips on how to plan your trip.

St. Martin's Island

St. Martin's Island is the only coral island in Bangladesh, and one of the most popular destinations for tourists. It is located about 9 km south of the mainland, and can be reached by boat from Teknaf. The island is small, only about 8 sq km, but it has a lot to offer. You can enjoy the crystal clear water, the coral reefs, the coconut groves, and the local seafood. You can also visit the neighboring Chera Dwip, a smaller island that is connected to St. Martin's by a sandbar during low tide. St. Martin's Island is a great place to relax and enjoy nature, but be aware that there is no electricity or fresh water on the island, so you will need to bring your own supplies or stay at a local guesthouse.

Sonadia Island

Sonadia Island is a large island, about 9 km long and 6 km wide, located near the mouth of the Bakkhali River. It is known for its mangrove forests, salt fields, and diverse wildlife. The island is home to many migratory birds, such as flamingos, herons, and ducks, as well as endangered species like the Irrawaddy dolphin and the olive ridley turtle. You can explore the island by boat, bicycle, or foot, and observe the local fishing and salt-making activities. You can also visit the nearby Moheshkhali Island, which has a famous Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Shakti.

Nijhum Dwip

Nijhum Dwip, or the Island of Silence, is a remote and tranquil island in the Bay of Bengal. It is part of the Noakhali district, and can be reached by boat from Hatiya or Sandwip. The island is about 16 km long and 10 km wide, and consists of several smaller islands that are separated by creeks. The island is mostly covered by forest, and is a sanctuary for deer, monkeys, and birds. You can enjoy the peaceful atmosphere, the green scenery, and the sandy beaches of the island, and also visit the local villages and markets. Nijhum Dwip is a perfect place to escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, and experience the rural life of Bangladesh.

Conclusion

Cox's Bazar Islands are some of the most beautiful and diverse places in Bangladesh, and they offer a unique opportunity for travelers to explore the natural and cultural aspects of the country. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway, an adventurous journey, or a cultural immersion, you will find something to suit your taste and budget on these islands. However, you should also be aware of the environmental and social challenges that these islands face, such as climate change, pollution, overfishing, and poverty, and try to be a responsible and respectful visitor. By doing so, you will not only have a memorable trip, but also contribute to the preservation and development of these islands.
